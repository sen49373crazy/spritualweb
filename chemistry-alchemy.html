⚗️ Chemistry & Alchemy

Ancient masters of transformation who understood the nature of matter, metals, and the alchemical processes of the universe

Nagarjuna

~150 - 250 CE

The father of Indian alchemy and chemistry, Nagarjuna wrote "Rasaratna Samuccaya" and "Rasendra Mangala", comprehensive texts on metallurgy, medicine preparation, and alchemical processes. He mastered the extraction and purification of metals.

Key Contributions:

  • Extraction of metals from ores
  • Preparation of mercury compounds
  • Medicinal chemistry and drug formulation
  • Distillation and sublimation techniques
  • Classification of minerals and metals

Acharya Kanad

~600 BCE

Beyond his atomic theory, Kanad described chemical reactions, the nature of chemical bonds, and how atoms combine to form molecules. His Vaisheshika philosophy laid the foundation for understanding matter transformation.

Key Contributions:

  • Atomic combinations (Dvyanuka - diatomic molecules)
  • Chemical reactions and transformations
  • Properties of elements and compounds
  • Heat and chemical change
  • Law of conservation in reactions

Charaka

~100 CE

Author of "Charaka Samhita", he extensively documented pharmaceutical chemistry, describing the preparation of medicinal compounds, extraction of plant essences, and chemical properties of herbs and minerals.

Key Contributions:

  • Pharmaceutical chemistry and drug preparation
  • Extraction and purification methods
  • Chemical properties of medicinal plants
  • Mineral-based medicines
  • Preservation and storage techniques

Vagbhata

~600 CE

A master of Rasa Shastra (mercurial chemistry), Vagbhata wrote extensively on the preparation of metallic and mineral medicines, detoxification processes, and the transformation of toxic substances into therapeutic agents.

Key Contributions:

  • Rasa Shastra (mercurial chemistry)
  • Detoxification of minerals and metals
  • Bhasma (calcination) preparations
  • Therapeutic use of metals
  • Chemical purification processes

Govinda Bhagavatpada

~700 CE

A renowned alchemist who wrote "Rasahridaya Tantra", detailing sophisticated chemical processes including calcination, sublimation, and the preparation of various chemical compounds for medicinal and metallurgical purposes.

Key Contributions:

  • Advanced calcination techniques
  • Sublimation and crystallization
  • Preparation of chemical compounds
  • Metallurgical processes
  • Alchemical transformation methods

Somadeva

~900 CE

Author of "Rasendra Chudamani", Somadeva documented extensive knowledge of chemical processes, including the preparation of various acids, alkalis, and their reactions with metals and minerals.

Key Contributions:

  • Preparation of acids and alkalis
  • Metal-acid reactions
  • Chemical apparatus and equipment
  • Systematic classification of chemicals
  • Laboratory techniques and safety
