Ancient sages who gazed at the cosmos and understood the laws of the universe, predating modern telescopes and physics

Aryabhata

476 - 550 CE

A pioneering astronomer who correctly explained solar and lunar eclipses, proposed the heliocentric model, and calculated the Earth's rotation period with remarkable accuracy. His astronomical observations were centuries ahead of his time.

Key Contributions:

  • Earth rotates on its axis (diurnal motion)
  • Correct explanation of eclipses
  • Sidereal period of Earth: 365.25858 days
  • Planetary positions and orbits
  • Reflected light theory for Moon and planets

Varahamihira

505 - 587 CE

Author of "Panchasiddhantika", Varahamihira synthesized five astronomical systems and made significant contributions to trigonometry, optics, and hydrology. He understood the relationship between the Moon and tides.

Key Contributions:

  • Comprehensive astronomical encyclopedia
  • Moon's influence on tides
  • Cometary studies and predictions
  • Atmospheric refraction
  • Water divining and hydrology

Bhaskara I

~600 - 680 CE

A brilliant astronomer and mathematician who wrote commentaries on Aryabhata's work and made original contributions to planetary motion, eclipses, and the sine function approximation.

Key Contributions:

  • Rational approximation of sine function
  • Planetary longitudes and positions
  • Eclipse calculations
  • Astronomical instruments
  • Heliocentric tendencies in calculations

Kanada (Kashyapa)

~600 BCE

The founder of Vaisheshika school of philosophy, Kanada proposed atomic theory 2,500 years before modern science. His "Vaisheshika Sutra" described the nature of matter, atoms (anu), and molecules (paramanu).

Key Contributions:

  • Atomic theory of matter (Paramanu)
  • Concept of indivisible particles
  • Laws of motion and gravity
  • Properties of matter and energy
  • Chemical combinations and reactions

Bhaskaracharya II

1114 - 1185 CE

In his astronomical work "Siddhanta Shiromani", Bhaskara calculated the time taken by Earth to orbit the Sun with incredible precision and described gravitational attraction centuries before Newton.

Key Contributions:

  • Earth's orbital period: 365.2588 days
  • Concept of gravitational attraction
  • Planetary positions and conjunctions
  • Spherical astronomy
  • Astronomical instruments design

Maharshi Kanad

~800 BCE

A visionary physicist-philosopher who described the nature of light, heat, and sound. His work on atomic structure and the nature of the universe laid foundations for physics millennia before modern discoveries.

Key Contributions:

  • Nature of light and heat
  • Sound as vibration
  • Concept of time and space
  • Causality and motion
  • Six categories of reality (Padarthas)
