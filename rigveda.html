Overview

The Oldest Divine Revelation

The Rigveda is the oldest of the four Vedas and one of the oldest extant texts in any Indo-European language. It is a collection of Vedic Sanskrit hymns (suktas) dedicated to Rigvedic deities. These sacred verses praise various cosmic forces, revealing the ancient seers' profound understanding of the universe, nature, and the divine.

The text is organized into ten books, known as Mandalas. The earliest books (Mandalas 2‚Äì7) are associated with specific rishi families. Mandala 1 and 10 are considered the youngest. Each Mandala consists of hymns (suktas), which in turn consist of individual verses (richas).

üåå Philosophical Significance

The Rigveda lays the foundation for all Hindu philosophy. It introduces key concepts like Rta (cosmic order), Dharma (duty/righteousness), and Satya (truth). It moves from polytheistic worship of nature deities to monistic questions about the origin of the universe.
