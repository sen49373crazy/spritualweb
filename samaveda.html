Overview

The Origin of Music

The Samaveda is the "Book of Chants" (Saman means "song" or "melody"). It consists of 1,549 verses, most of which are taken from the Rigveda but are set to musical notation for singing during rituals. It represents the origin of Indian classical music and the science of sound vibration (Nada Brahma).

üéº Structure & Organization

The Samaveda is divided into two main parts: the Purvarchika (collection of melodies) and the Uttararchika (collection of chants for rituals). The text functions essentially as a songbook for the Udgatr priests who chanted during major sacrifices.

üéπ Contribution to Music

It is the earliest known text to structure sound into musical notes. The seven notes of the Indian scale (Sa, Re, Ga, Ma, Pa, Dha, Ni) originated here. It emphasizes that sound (Shabda) is a path to spiritual realization, laying the groundwork for Mantra Yoga.
