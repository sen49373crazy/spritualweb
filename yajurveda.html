Overview

The Guide to Ceremonial Action

The Yajurveda contains prose mantras and formulas for performing yajnas (fire sacrifices). It provides detailed instructions for rituals and ceremonies, bridging the gap between spiritual knowledge and practical application. It is the manual for the Adhvaryu priest who performs the physical details of the ritual.

Shukla (White) Yajurveda: Contains only the mantras (prayers) to be recited. The explanations are in separate Brahmana texts.

Krishna (Black) Yajurveda: Contains the mantras mixed with prose commentary and instructions within the same text.

The Yajurveda contributes to the science of geometry (Sulba Sutras) through the precise construction of fire altars. It also details the measurement of time and the systematic organization of complex ceremonies, laying the groundwork for mathematics and architecture.
